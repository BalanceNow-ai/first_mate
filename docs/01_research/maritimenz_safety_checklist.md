# Maritime NZ Boat Safety Equipment Checklist (Official)
Source: https://www.maritimenz.govt.nz/media/cv4hghzw/boat-safety-equipment-checklist-final.pdf
Recommended by: Coastguard NZ, Drowning Prevention Auckland, NZ Marine Industry Association

## Equipment List (Trailer Power and Sail Boats)

### Boat Name and Identification
- Identifiable by name on both sides, legible from 50m. Required in some regions.

### Navigation Lights
- Port (red) and starboard (green) navigation lights for night use
- White all-round anchor light

### Anchors and Chains
- Anchor suitable for boat size
- Chain at least 1.5x vessel length
- Anchor warp minimum 50 metres
- Spare anchor, chain, and warp recommended

### Docking Lines & Boat Hook
- Lines 1.5x boat length, 10mm diameter, spliced looped ends
- Suitable boat hook

### Fire Extinguishers
- Under 6m: 2kg portable
- 6m to 12m: 2.5kg portable
- Petrol inboard engines: working bilge blower required

### First Aid Kit
- A suitable first aid kit must be available

### Life Jackets
- Suitable lifejackets for all persons aboard
- Nationwide requirement to wear when underway unless skipper deems safe not to
- Some regional bylaws: must be worn on boats 6m or less at all times

### Navigation Equipment and Charts
- Fixed or handheld compass recommended
- GPS navigation unit fixed to vessel
- Mobile device with navigation app (e.g., Navionics)
- Charts for the area being navigated

### Bailing Equipment
- At least one bucket
- Working bilge pump (vessels 6m and above)

### Robust Knife/Hacksaw

### Torch/Flashlight
- Torch powered independently of boat batteries
- Spare torch or batteries

### At Least Two Communication Devices
- VHF marine radio (registered with call sign recommended)
- Suitable loud horn
- Cell phone in watertight bag
AND:
- EPIRB or PLB (all beacons must be registered at beacons.org.nz)
- Two currently dated red distress flares + one daytime orange smoke flare in watertight container
