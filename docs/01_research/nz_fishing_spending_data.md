# NZ Recreational Fishing Spending Data (Southwick et al., Fisheries Research 2018)

## Source: "Estimating marine recreational fishing's economic contributions in New Zealand"
Fisheries Research 208 (2018) 116–123

## Key NZ Market Size Stats
- 595,662 NZ residents 15+ fished marine waters in 2011-12
- 2.652 million fishing trips per year
- 4.5 fishing days per fisher per year
- NZ hosted 2.52 million international visitors in 2014, with 108,811 (4%) taking part in marine fishing

## Table 1: Direct Spending Profiles of Resident Fishers (NZ$)

### New Zealand marine fishers trip and equipment spending:
- **Total trip-related spending per day: $99.45**
  - Food and lodging: 38%
  - Transport: 22%
  - Other: 22% (remaining)

- **Total equipment item spending per year: $998.04**
  - **Fishing equipment: 17%** (~$170 per fisher per year)
  - **Boats & vehicles: 31%** (~$309 per fisher per year)
  - **Accessories: 38%** (~$379 per fisher per year)
  - Other: 14%

## Key Insight for SKU Planning:
- NZ resident fishers spend ~$998/year on fishing equipment and related items
- **Accessories are the largest equipment spend category at 38%** (~$379/fisher/year)
- Boats & vehicles at 31%, fishing equipment at 17%
- With ~595,662 active marine fishers → total equipment spend ≈ $594M NZD/year
- This is 2014/15 data; with inflation and growth, 2025 figure likely $800M-$1B+ NZD

## Implication:
The accessories category (hooks, line, lures, sinkers, tackle boxes, rods, reels, safety gear, electronics) 
represents the highest volume and value opportunity for a NZ marine/fishing e-commerce platform.
