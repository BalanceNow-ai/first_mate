# NZ Competitor Audit Notes

## Marine Deals (marine-deals.co.nz / www.marine-deals.co.nz)
- Positioning: "NZ's Largest Online Superstore" - fishing, boating, camping, clothing, watersports, diving, hunting, RV
- 21,000+ products in stock
- Key value props: Same-day shipping (NZ Post, 95% orders), Full Warranty, No-Worries Returns, Lowest Price Guarantee
- Navigation: Category-based (Fishing, Boating, Camping, Clothing, Watersports, Diving, Hunting, RV, Gift Ideas, Cool Stuff)
- Shop By Brand available
- Loyalty VIP Club exists
- Trade account available
- Has an Australian store
- Supports Coastguard NZ
- Articles section (basic content)
- Click and Collect available
- Currency selector (NZD)
- Wishlist feature

### GAPS/WEAKNESSES (Marine Deals):
- No vessel/engine fitment tool ("My Vessel" / garage concept)
- No AI features whatsoever
- No interactive parts diagrams
- No community forum or expert community
- No specialised domain knowledge agents
- Very generic "superstore" positioning - not specialist marine/fishing
- Content/articles section appears basic
- No personalisation beyond basic account
- No installation guides or service provider directory
- No fishing spot / local knowledge integration
- Product range is broad but shallow in specialist marine parts

---

## Burnsco (burnsco.co.nz)
- Positioning: Boating, Motorhome, Fishing & more
- Has physical stores (nationwide store network)
- Key value props: Price Match Policy, Free 1-Hour Click & Collect, Nationwide Store Network, Free Standard Delivery Over $99, AfterPay available
- Navigation: Products, Brands, Clearance, Guides and Advice, Burnsco Trade
- Has a "Guides and Advice" section with articles on fishing techniques, safety, electrical, motorhome
- Has Burnsco Trade account
- Store Locator
- Wishlist
- Sign In / Sign Up
- AfterPay available

### GAPS/WEAKNESSES (Burnsco):
- No vessel/engine fitment tool
- No AI features
- No interactive parts diagrams
- No community forum
- Guides are basic blog articles, not integrated with product recommendations
- No personalisation
- No service provider directory
- No fishing spot integration
- Physical store focus means online experience is secondary
- No specialised domain agents

---

## Smart Marine (smartmarine.co.nz)
- Positioning: Boating, Fishing & Outdoors
- Has physical stores (multiple locations)
- Key value props: Price Promise (price match), Click & Collect (ready in 90 mins), Free Shipping over $200, Smart VIP Club
- Navigation: BBQs & Smokers, Boating, Clothing & Footwear, Diving, Fishing, Marine Electronics, RV & Outdoors, Clearance, New, Shop by Brand
- Smart VIP Card with member pricing (two-tier pricing visible on products)
- AfterPay / Laybuy available (4 payments shown on products)
- Social media active (Instagram @SmartMarineNZ)
- Has a "Price Promise" badge prominently displayed
- Basic search

### GAPS/WEAKNESSES (Smart Marine):
- Very cluttered, visually busy homepage (lots of banner ads, promotional graphics)
- No vessel/engine fitment tool
- No AI features
- No interactive parts diagrams
- No community forum
- No content/guides section visible
- No personalisation
- No service provider directory
- Design feels dated and promotional-heavy
- No specialised domain agents

---

## COMMON GAPS ACROSS ALL THREE NZ COMPETITORS:
1. No vessel/boat profile ("My Vessel" garage concept)
2. No AI-powered assistance of any kind
3. No interactive OEM parts diagrams or schematics
4. No community forum or expert community
5. No specialised domain knowledge (pumps, galley, fenders, game fishing, etc.)
6. No personalised recommendations based on vessel or customer history
7. No service provider / installer directory
8. No fishing spot / local knowledge integration
9. No installation guides integrated with product pages
10. No proactive maintenance reminders
11. No project management / wishlist-to-project workflow
12. No NZ-specific regulatory compliance guidance (Maritime NZ rules, safety requirements)
13. No video content / expert reviews
14. No RAG-powered search or conversational product discovery
